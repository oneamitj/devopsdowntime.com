DevOps Downtime is a meetup event aimed at bringing together IT professionals to share knowledge and experiences relating to DevOps, SRE, Security, and Operations. The event is open to everyone and can be hosted by any interested organisation.

We want to unleash a wave of understanding about DevOps as a career path and provide a common ground for all IT personnel to come together, share experiences, problems and find solutions — meeting like-minded people in the field.

Our goal is to build a community that fosters learning, collaboration, and innovation, supporting DevOps professionals at every stage of their careers — from seasoned veterans to those just starting out.

Talk Topics

What makes a great
DevOps Downtime talk?

Talks should be a teaser of a technology or concept the speaker is passionate about — not a comprehensive workshop. Personal experience is key.

Great topics

  • Infrastructure as Code (IaC) with Terraform
  • CI/CD with Azure DevOps or GitHub Actions
  • Container Orchestration with Kubernetes or Docker Swarm
  • Best practices for monitoring and logging
  • Introduction to GitOps
  • Automating infrastructure with Ansible
  • Serverless computing with AWS Lambda
  • Managing Secrets and Credentials in DevOps
  • Exploring the benefits of DevSecOps

Avoid these

  • The history of DevOps
  • General comparison of DevOps tools (e.g., Jenkins vs CircleCI)
  • General introduction to cloud computing (e.g., What is AWS?)
  • Introduction to programming languages
  • The basics of networking (TCP/IP, DNS)
Hosting Guidelines

Running a DevOps Downtime event

The venue host decides the format, finds speakers, and handles event marketing — while following these core guidelines to keep the experience consistent.

1

Formal talks should be 30 minutes max, followed by an open informal networking session.

2

Creatives must follow the DevOps Downtime brand guidelines and should not include the host company's logo or branding.

3

Always mention the day of the week (Saturday/Sunday) on every creative asset and poster.

4

Put up proper signage on the nearby road and along the path to the venue on the day of the event.

5

No expenses on lunch. Only tea, coffee, and light snacks are permitted.

6

Attendees are never charged for tickets or snacks. The event is always free to attend.
