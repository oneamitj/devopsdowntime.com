About Us

Nepal's overall Ops
community floor

DevOps Downtime is a grass-roots community meetup for engineers in Nepal. We bypass corporate sales pitches and marketing overhead to focus entirely on real engineering experiences, sharing production lessons, and studying cloud as well as on-premise architectures.

Whether you are an automated-everything veteran or just getting started with containers and orchestration, our meetups provide a shared community floor to network, brainstorm solutions to common operational challenges, and build professional connections.

"We envisioned a space where engineers can talk shop, share production lessons, and connect with their peers without corporate sales noise."

— Organizers
Our Core Values

What DevOps Downtime stands for

We build each session around simple, transparent engineering values rather than rigid event structures.

01

Knowledge Over Marketing

100% focused on engineering facts, metrics, post-mortems, and technical architectures. Strict zero-sales-pitch policy.

02

Peer-to-Peer Exchange

A shared, open floor where seasoned architects and curious juniors discuss systems as equals. Everyone has a voice.

03

Real-World Engineering

No theoretical fluff. Every session explores actual deployment scripts, container outages, or live terminal sessions.

04

Chill Downtime Vibe

Ample networking space dedicated after technical presentations to wind down, share production horror stories, and connect.

Talk & Speakers

What makes a great Downtime talk?

Downtime talks are highly focused engineering show-and-tell sessions. We value transparent technical stories, live console sharing, and lessons learned from failure.

Technical Authenticity

  • Raw production post-mortems of real system failures.
  • Practical blueprints and configurations used in production.
  • Live console walkthroughs and running code blocks.
  • Actionable takeaways other engineers can apply immediately.

Tradeoffs & Focus

  • Focus on the "Why" and tradeoffs over hype.
  • Deep technical metrics over high-level marketing talk.
  • Sharing genuine experiences instead of generic slides.
  • Sparking interactive debate and direct Q&A with peers.
